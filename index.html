Darjeeling in March: Where Spring Meets the Himalayas

As winter gently fades, Darjeeling transforms into a refreshing hill escape in March —offering crisp mountain air, blooming rhododendrons, and clear views of snow-capped peaks.

It’s the perfect time to experience the charm of this serene destination before the busy tourist season begins.

Things to pack for the trip

  • ID Proof (Aadhar/Voter ID): Required for hotel check-ins and for permits if you plan to visit nearby restricted areas like North Sikkim.
  • Motion Sickness Medicines :The road from Siliguri/Bagdogra to Darjeeling has over 100 hair-pin bends. Even if you don't usually get sick, it’s good to have.
  • Sweaters or hoodies for the morning and evenings :You'll be experiencing that crisp Himalayan spring air with a chance of light rain, your packing list needs to balance warmth and waterproofing.
  • Sturdy Walking Shoes: Darjeeling is all steep slopes and cobblestones. Bring shoes with excellent grip (and ideally water-resistance).

Travel Itinerary

Date Place Activity
March 15, 2026 Arrival & Mall Road Arrive via NJP; check into hotel. Spend a relaxed evening walking through the vehicle-free Chowrasta (Mall Road) and enjoy sunset views from Observatory Hill.
March 16, 2026 Tiger Hill & Local Sites 4:00 AM drive to Tiger Hill for the famous sunrise over Mt. Kanchenjunga. Visit Ghoom Monastery and Batasia Loop on the way back. After breakfast, visit the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute and Darjeeling Zoo.
March 17, 2026 Tea & Toy Train Morning tour of Happy Valley Tea Estate to see tea processing. Afternoon UNESCO Toy Train Joyride from Darjeeling to Ghoom. Visit the Japanese Peace Pagoda for panoramic views.
March 18, 2026 Offbeat Day Trip Full-day excursion to Mirik Lake for boating through pine forests. Stop at Pashupati Market (Indo-Nepal border) and Simana Viewpoint for mountain vistas.
March 19, 2026 Rock Garden & Departure Visit the terraced Barbotey Rock Garden and Ganga Maya Park featuring natural waterfalls. Afternoon departure to Bagdogra/NJP via the scenic Kurseong route.
